Memory prices are spiking — and PC vendors are already warning you

After years of cheap DRAM and SSDs, the pendulum swung hard: analysts report memory prices up as much as 90% since late 2025, and custom PC builders like PowerGPU say they’ll raise system prices once their old inventory is gone. This isn’t a one‑off sale ending; it’s a new phase of the DRAM/NAND cycle, powered by AI/datacenter demand and earlier production cuts. If you’ve been procrastinating on a RAM or SSD upgrade, the market’s basically telling you it’s not getting cheaper any time soon.

Epstein files: the 'pizza' code-word story, minus the hysteria

A DOJ document dump shows the word "pizza" appearing 859 times, and parts of the internet immediately decided it must be a secret code. The actual article: some uses look like normal food talk, some are unsettling, and the piece itself ends with "we don't really know".

What the Innocence Project actually does

The Innocence Project works to exonerate people who were wrongfully convicted, often using DNA testing and new evidence to overturn cases built on junk forensics, coerced confessions, or bad eyewitness IDs. Their case list is hundreds of names long, and each one is a reminder that "the system" is only as good as the science, lawyers, and incentives we give it.
